# MASTER CYBERSECURITY FLASHCARD DECK
**STATUS:** ACTIVE
**PURPOSE:** Rapid Recall Training

---

<details>
  <summary><b>CARD 1: What is the CIA Triad? (Tap to reveal)</b></summary>
  <br>
  <i>ANSWER:</i> The foundational model of cybersecurity standing for Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability.
</details>

<hr>

<details>
  <summary><b>CARD 2: What is a Zero-Day Exploit? (Tap to reveal)</b></summary>
  <br>
  <i>ANSWER:</i> A cyber attack that occurs on the same day a weakness is discovered in software, meaning the developer has had "zero days" to patch or fix it.
</details>

<hr>

<details>
  <summary><b>CARD 3: Define Phishing. (Tap to reveal)</b></summary>
  <br>
  <i>ANSWER:</i> A social engineering attack where a threat actor impersonates a trusted entity to deceive a victim into revealing sensitive information or deploying malicious software.
</details>
<details>
  <summary><b>CARD 4: What is the Principle of Least Privilege (PoLP)? (Tap to reveal)</b></summary>
  <br>
  <i>ANSWER:</i> A security architecture rule stating that a user, program, or process should only have the bare minimum privileges necessary to perform its intended function.
</details>

<hr>

<details>
  <summary><b>CARD 5: Explain the difference between Symmetric and Asymmetric Encryption. (Tap to reveal)</b></summary>
  <br>
  <i>ANSWER:</i> Symmetric encryption uses the SAME key to lock and unlock the data (faster, but harder to share the key safely). Asymmetric encryption uses a public key to lock the data and a separate private key to unlock it (slower, but highly secure for transmission).
</details>

<hr>

<details>
  <summary><b>CARD 6: What is a DDoS Attack? (Tap to reveal)</b></summary>
  <br>
  <i>ANSWER:</i> Distributed Denial of Service. A threat actor uses a botnet (a swarm of compromised computers) to flood a target server with overwhelming traffic, causing it to crash and breaching the 'Availability' pillar of the CIA Triad.
</details>

<hr>

<details>
  <summary><b>CARD 7: Define Social Engineering. (Tap to reveal)</b></summary>
  <br>
  <i>ANSWER:</i> Manipulating human psychology to bypass technical security measures. Instead of hacking the firewall, the attacker tricks an employee into handing over the password.
</details>

<hr>

<details>
  <summary><b>CARD 8: What is the difference between a Vulnerability Scan and a Penetration Test? (Tap to reveal)</b></summary>
  <br>
  <i>ANSWER:</i> A Vulnerability Scan is an automated tool that looks for known weaknesses. A Penetration Test (Pen Test) is an authorized, manual simulated cyberattack by a human engineer to exploit weaknesses and test the actual defensive posture.
</details>

<hr>

<details>
  <summary><b>CARD 9: What is Ransomware? (Tap to reveal)</b></summary>
  <br>
  <i>ANSWER:</i> Malicious software that encrypts a victim's files or locks them out of their network entirely. The threat actor then demands financial compensation (usually cryptocurrency) in exchange for the decryption key.
</details>

<hr>

<details>
  <summary><b>CARD 10: What is MFA (Multi-Factor Authentication)? (Tap to reveal)</b></summary>
  <br>
  <i>ANSWER:</i> An access control system requiring two or more distinct pieces of evidence. It combines something you know (password), something you have (a phone for an SMS code/authenticator app), and/or something you are (biometrics/fingerprint).
</details>
